Getting here

Hello!

My ham journey started in 2018, when a colleague and friend at work got my interest piqued in using ham radio for emergency preparedness. I decided to check it out, ended up taking a class offered through the local club (Clark County Amateur Radio Club, W7AIA), and received my Technician License in Nov of 2018 as KJ7BSP.

Not quite sure what to do once I had it, I sat on my license for almost a year before deciding to attend a club meeting. I was quite surprised to show up at that first meeting and discover so many people coming together to share their love of amateur radio.

At the club meetings, I was encouraged to start participating in the local VHF/UHF nets, and had several conversations with club members who hinted at the glory of HF.

So, I checked out three of the local VHF/UHF nets and discovered one was for the Clark County ARES/RACES group. I was curious about their mission, so looked into it, attended a meeting in the latter half of 2019, and soon volunteered to become a member. I now serve on the tactical team loosely based in North Clark County, WA.

Another of the local nets was for the EYEWARN group. I started participating in it regularly as well, believed in what they are doing, and became an EYEWARN net control operator in mid-2020.

Harkening back to the hints of HF glory, I soon found myself studying the General License manual and tested for that in Feb of 2020. I assumed the vanity callsign K7BPM in Apr of 2021.

Ham radio has also given me the opportunity to volunteer in our local food drive. I have been able to participate in the regional Walk and Knock Food Drive since 2019 as a volunteer communicator. The event is in early December and really does a lot of good for the local food bank.

I also develop the website for the Lewis & Clark Trail on the Air special event. June 2022 was its inaugural year. 2026 will be our fifth go round.

At home, I currently run an Icom ID-5100 through a Comet CX-333 for my VHF/UHF base station. While my base radio is only a dual band, I have a Yaesu VX-6R tri-band HT that I occasionally use on 220.

I obtained an Icom 7300 in Dec 2021 and have been exploring many of the digital modes and continuing to learn as much as I can.

In June 2023 I finished the “Intermediate” level CWOps course through the CW Academy — shout out to my advisors: Beginner Course: KD2KW & AC5EZ, Fundamental Course: K1DW & K1EBY, and Intermediate Course: K7MOZ — you are all awesome. Life got busy and I set it aside for a while, but in 2026 I’m back at it. I’ve made a public goal of 100 CW QSOs this year. I had exactly 2 lifetime CW QSOs to my name prior to the goal — plenty of room to grow.

I've got several other antenna projects in the works, needing various levels of elbow grease.

Outside of radio stuff — I'm not retired yet, and work as a web application software developer (primarily Ruby on Rails). At home, I like to tinker on things and spend time with my wife and dogs.

Hope to catch you on the air!

73 de K7BPM
